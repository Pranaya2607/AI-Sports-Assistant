# Sports Equipment Knowledge Base

This file is used by the RAG module. You can add extra PDFs, rulebooks, brand manuals, and maintenance guides in this folder.

## Cricket Bat
A cricket bat is used to strike the cricket ball. It is usually made from English willow or Kashmir willow. English willow is premium, lighter, and preferred by advanced players. Kashmir willow is more affordable and durable for beginners. Maintenance includes knocking-in, oiling lightly, using a toe guard, storing away from moisture, and using an anti-scuff sheet.

## Cricket Ball
A cricket ball is made with a cork core, wound thread, and leather cover. Hard leather balls can cause injury, so players need helmets, pads, gloves, and other protective gear. A damaged seam or cracked leather reduces performance.

## Football
A football is an inflated spherical ball used in association football. Size 5 is commonly used by adults, size 4 by youth players, and size 3 by younger children. Correct pressure, surface suitability, and panel quality affect performance.

## Basketball
Basketballs are made for indoor or outdoor surfaces. Indoor balls often use composite leather. Outdoor balls use stronger rubber. Size 7 is common for men, size 6 for women, and size 5 for young players. Good grip and correct inflation are important.

## Volleyball
Volleyballs are lighter than many other sports balls and are used for serving, receiving, setting, and spiking. Indoor volleyballs and beach volleyballs differ in weight, covering, and durability.

## Tennis Racket
Tennis racket selection depends on grip size, head size, weight, balance, and string tension. Beginners usually need a lightweight racket with a larger head for more forgiveness. Advanced players may choose heavier rackets for control.

## Badminton Racket
Badminton rackets are lightweight and may be made from graphite, carbon fiber, or aluminum. String tension affects power and control. Beginners should avoid very high tension because it reduces the sweet spot.

## Shuttlecock
Feather shuttlecocks provide better flight but wear faster. Nylon shuttlecocks last longer and are suitable for training. Broken feathers or cracked cork reduce flight stability.

## Hockey Stick
Hockey stick length, bow shape, weight, and material affect control and power. Carbon fiber provides power, while wooden or fiberglass sticks may offer better touch for beginners. Shin guards and mouthguards are important.

## Baseball Bat
Baseball bats may be wooden, aluminum, or composite. Proper weight and length are important for swing control. Cracked wooden bats and dented metal bats should not be used.

## Baseball Glove
Baseball gloves are made from leather or synthetic material and need proper breaking in. Laces, pocket shape, and padding should be inspected regularly. Do not dry leather gloves using direct heat.

## Goalkeeper Gloves
Goalkeeper gloves use latex palms for grip and foam padding for protection. Training gloves prioritize durability, while match gloves prioritize grip. Wash gently and dry naturally after play.

## Helmet
A sports helmet protects the head and sometimes the face. Fit, certification, padding, grille, and chin strap are important. Replace helmets after strong impact or visible cracks.

## Golf Club
Golf clubs include drivers, woods, irons, wedges, and putters. Club length, loft, shaft flex, and grip size affect performance. Beginners usually prefer forgiving clubs with larger sweet spots.

## Sports Shoes
Sports shoes should match sport-specific movements. Running shoes focus on cushioning, basketball shoes on ankle stability, football boots on studs and traction, and court shoes on lateral movement. Worn soles increase injury risk.

## Accessory Recommendation Principles
Essential accessories improve usability and safety. Maintenance accessories extend product life. Examples include pumps for balls, grips for rackets and bats, covers for bats and clubs, pads and guards for impact sports, and cleaning kits for shoes and gloves.

## Fake Equipment Detection Principles
Fake sports equipment often has unusually low prices, poor stitching, bad finishing, wrong fonts, inconsistent logos, weak packaging, no invoice, missing QR or serial number, and sellers who avoid warranty proof. Verification should be done through authorized dealers, brand websites, serial checks, and invoice validation.

## Maintenance Principles
Keep equipment clean and dry. Avoid direct heat. Use covers during storage. Inflate balls correctly. Replace worn grips, strings, soles, or cracked parts. Safety equipment should be replaced after major impact.
